# Foster Greatness Master Brand System

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive guidance for creating authentic, empowering content and designs for Foster Greatness. It ensures consistency across all touchpoints while maintaining the dignity-centered, lived experience-led approach that defines the organization.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill for **ANY** Foster Greatness project including:
- Website and digital platform design
- Marketing and communications materials
- Partnership proposals and presentations
- Event materials and collateral
- Internal documents and templates
- Email campaigns and newsletters
- Grant applications and impact reports
- Print materials and merchandise
- Video and multimedia content
- Community resources and guides

## Organization Identity

### Mission & Core Philosophy

**Mission:** Foster Greatness is a lived experience-led nonprofit platform creating lifelong community and belonging for current and former foster youth nationwide.

**Core Philosophy:** "Age into but never age out" - We provide comprehensive support that evolves with members throughout their entire lives, not just during transition periods.

**The Why:** Every foster youth deserves not just a job, housing, or an education, but a lifelong community. We built a business model to make that possible.

### Core Values

**Lived-Experience Led**
- Built by and for people impacted by foster care
- Member voices drive every decision
- Authentic understanding of the journey

**Innovation**
- Business-powered sustainability through DGW Branded
- Digital-first community platform
- Scalable support systems

**Empowerment**
- Dignity-centered approach always
- Strengths-based language and resources
- Member agency and choice prioritized

### What Sets Foster Greatness Apart

1. **Movement, Not Charity** - Creating spaces where belonging fuels transformation
2. **Lived Experience Leadership** - Built for foster youth, by foster youth
3. **Business-Powered Sustainability** - DGW Branded partnership provides stable funding
4. **Community Ownership** - Members actively shape programs and direction
5. **Dignity-Centered Resources** - Support positioned as earned, never given
6. **Lifelong Commitment** - No age-out, support evolves with members

### The DGW Branded Partnership

**Critical Context:** DGW Branded (Doing Good Works) is the promotional products social enterprise that powers Foster Greatness through direct business revenue. This isn't traditional fundraising—it's sustainable business as a force for good.

**Partnership Message:** "Transform your routine business purchases into life-changing impact. Every promotional product order directly funds scholarships, crisis support, and career development for foster youth."

## Brand Voice & Language

### Voice Personality

Foster Greatness speaks as a **trusted friend who's been there**—someone who understands the journey, celebrates the victories, and stands ready during the challenges.

**Core Characteristics:**

**Authentic**
- Real experiences, real stories
- Never performative or scripted
- Honest about challenges while focusing on strengths
- Contemporary language, not institutional

**Empowering**
- Strengths-based language always
- Focus on resilience and potential
- Agency and choice in every message
- Active voice that gives power

**Conversational**
- Warm and approachable
- Free from institutional jargon
- Like talking to a peer who gets it
- "We know," "we're here," "you've got this"

**Inclusive**
- Welcoming to all foster care experiences
- Recognizing intersectional identities
- Multiple paths to engagement
- No judgment, all support

**Professional yet Personal**
- Credible without being cold
- Data-driven but human-centered
- Business-minded with heart
- Partners trust us, members feel us

### Language Framework: Word Choices Matter

#### ALWAYS Use These Terms

| ❌ Never Use | ✅ Always Use | Why |
|-------------|--------------|-----|
| Beneficiary, client, recipient | Community member | Emphasizes belonging, not transaction |
| Help, assistance, aid | Support you deserve | Rights-based, not charity-based |
| Charity, donation, handout | Community investment | Mutual benefit, not one-way |
| Services, programs | Resources, opportunities | Opens possibilities, not prescribed paths |
| Aged out | Former foster youth, foster alumni | Respectful, identity-affirming language |
| At-risk, vulnerable, disadvantaged | Youth with lived experience | Strengths-based, removes deficit framing |
| Broken system | Systemic barriers | Acknowledges reality without hopelessness |
| Fix, save, rescue | Support, empower, connect | Removes savior complex |
| Less fortunate | Foster youth navigating challenges | Acknowledges circumstances without pity |
| Case management | Resource navigation | Removes clinical/institutional tone |
| Give back | Invest in community | Partnership, not charity |

#### Power Words to Embrace

Use these words intentionally throughout all content:

- **Belonging** - Our core offering and what everyone deserves
- **Community** - What we create together
- **Transform** - What happens here through connection
- **Navigate** - How we support the journey
- **Thrive** - Our goal for every member
- **Connect** - What brings us together
- **Empower** - Our approach to all programs
- **Celebrate** - How we recognize every success
- **Choose/Choice** - Agency in action
- **Deserve** - Rights-based framing, not charity
- **Lifelong** - Our commitment that never expires
- **Platform** - Modern, tech-forward positioning
- **Peer** - The power of shared experience

#### Never Use These Terms

❌ Deficit-based language (at-risk, disadvantaged, vulnerable, broken)
❌ Charity narratives (help the less fortunate, give back, reach out to)
❌ Savior language (rescue, fix, save, help them)
❌ Institutional jargon (client services, case management, intervention)
❌ Passive voice that removes agency ("help is provided" vs "you can access")
❌ Generic "support" without specificity
❌ Transactional language (donor, beneficiary, recipient)
❌ Temporary framing (transitional, short-term, temporary)

**For Current Foster Youth (14-21)**
- Peer-to-peer energy and contemporary language
- Emphasis on immediate, tangible resources
- "This is YOUR space" - ownership language
- Hope without toxic positivity
- Direct, authentic communication
- Example: "Whether you need help with housing, want to share your story, or just need to know you're not alone—we've got you."

**For Foster Alumni (22+)**
- Professional empowerment focus
- Leadership and advancement opportunities
- "Your experience is expertise" messaging
- Career and life advancement focus
- Peer leadership positioning
- Example: "Your journey through foster care gave you skills that employers need. Let's put them to work."

**For Corporate Partners**
- ROI and business value focus
- Innovation in social impact
- Measurable outcomes and data
- Professional credibility
- Sustainable business model emphasis
- Example: "Transform routine business purchases into measurable social impact through our innovative partnership model."

**For Individual Supporters**
- Community participation, not charity
- Transparent impact and outcomes
- Multiple engagement options
- Movement membership mentality
- Example: "Join a movement creating lifelong belonging for foster youth nationwide."

**For Media**
- Data-driven narratives with human stories
- Systems change focus
- Lived experience expertise
- Innovation in nonprofit space
- Example: "Foster Greatness is disrupting traditional nonprofit models through business-powered sustainability and lived experience leadership."

### Writing Guidelines

**Sentence Structure**
- Use active voice: "Members access resources" not "Resources are provided"
- Keep sentences clear and concise (15-20 words average)
- Lead with the most important information
- Use contractions for conversational tone (we're, you're, it's)

**Paragraph Structure**
- One main idea per paragraph
- 2-4 sentences ideal for digital content
- Generous white space for readability
- Use bullet points for lists and multiple ideas

**Call-to-Action Language**
- Direct and specific: "Create Your Free Account" not "Learn More"
- Low barrier language: "Sign up in less than 2 minutes"
- Multiple pathways: "Join us, Connect with peers, Access resources"
- No pressure: "When you're ready" positioning

**Storytelling Approach**
- Lead with emotional connection, follow with data
- Use real member quotes with consent
- Show transformation, not tragedy
- Focus on community impact, not individual rescue
- Example structure: Challenge → Community Support → Outcome → Call to Action

- Use for: Highlights, energy accents, optimistic moments

- Accessibility: Moderate contrast - 2.98:1 ratio (use for large elements)

**Teal to Navy** - Primary brand gradient for headers and hero sections
**Navy to Black** - Professional, sophisticated for business materials
**Light Blue to Accent Blue** - Soft, approachable for community content

### Color Usage Guidelines

**Accessibility Requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA)**
- Body text minimum contrast: 4.5:1
- Large text (18pt+/14pt+ bold) minimum: 3:1
- UI components and graphics: 3:1
- Always test colors for colorblind accessibility

**Best Practices:**
- Primary Navy Blue (#1a2949) for all body text on light backgrounds
- Primary Teal Blue (#0067a2) for clickable links and CTAs
- White (#ffffff) text exclusively on Navy backgrounds
- Never use accent colors for body text
- Test yellow/orange accents as highlights only, never for essential information
- Maintain brand colors consistently across all platforms

**Color Combinations to Avoid:**
- ❌ Yellow text on white backgrounds
- ❌ Light Blue text on any background
- ❌ Accent Blue for important text
- ❌ Orange on Navy (insufficient contrast)

### Typography

#### Primary Font Family: Century Gothic

**Why Century Gothic:**
- The definitive Foster Greatness typeface
- Clean, geometric sans-serif
- Modern and approachable without being trendy
- Excellent readability across sizes
- Professional yet friendly
- Strong brand recognition

#### Font Hierarchy & Sizes

**Digital/Web:**
- H1: 48-64px (Bold) - Hero headlines, landing pages
- H2: 36-42px (Bold) - Major section headers
- H3: 28-32px (Semibold) - Subsection headers
- H4: 24px (Semibold) - Card headers, minor sections
- Body: 16-18px (Regular) - All body copy, optimal readability
- Caption: 14px (Light or Regular) - Image captions, footnotes, legal
- Button Text: 16-18px (Bold) - CTAs and buttons

**Print:**
- Headlines: 24-48pt (Bold)
- Subheadings: 18-20pt (Regular or Semibold)
- Body: 12-14pt (Regular)
- Impact Numbers: 60pt+ (Bold)

#### Typography Rules

**Readability Standards:**
- Line height: 1.5-1.7 for body text (optimal readability)
- Maximum line length: 75 characters (50-75 ideal)
- Paragraph spacing: 1.5x line height minimum
- Letter spacing: Default (do not condense or expand)

**Alignment:**
- Left-align body text for readability
- Center-align only for short headlines or calls-to-action
- Never justify text (creates uneven spacing)

**Emphasis:**
- Use bold for emphasis, avoid italics (harder to read)
- Use color sparingly for emphasis
- Never use all caps for body text
- Use sentence case for headlines (not title case)

**Accessibility:**
- Minimum 16px for body text on digital
- Minimum 12pt for body text in print
- Ensure sufficient contrast (see color guidelines)
- Avoid text in images when possible (use HTML text)
- Provide alt text for any text-in-image content

**Font Loading & Fallbacks:**
- Primary: Century Gothic
- Fallback: Futura, 'Century Gothic', AppleGothic, sans-serif
- Always specify fallback fonts for web
- Test font rendering across browsers and devices

### Logo Usage

#### Logo Variations

**Primary Logo**
- "FOSTER ❤️ GREATNESS" with blue heart symbol
- Horizontal layout with consistent spacing
- Use for: Main brand applications, headers, marketing
- Available in: Full color, navy, white/reversed

**Secondary Logo**
- Stacked version: "FOSTER ❤️" on top, "GREATNESS" below
- Use when: Horizontal space is limited (mobile, vertical layouts)
- Maintains heart symbol prominence

**Icon Version**
- "FG❤️" abbreviated with heart
- "FG" in square badge format
- Use for: Favicons, app icons, small applications, social media avatars
- Minimum size: 32x32px

#### Logo Rules & Clear Space

**Required Clear Space:**
- Minimum clear space: Height of the "F" on all sides
- Never place logo against busy backgrounds without clear space
- Use solid background or subtle gradient only

**What NEVER to Do:**
- ❌ Never stretch, skew, or alter proportions
- ❌ Never change colors outside brand palette
- ❌ Never add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines, glows)
- ❌ Never rotate the logo
- ❌ Never place on low-contrast backgrounds
- ❌ Never recreate or retype the logo
- ❌ Never separate the heart from the wordmark

**Background Usage:**
- Light backgrounds: Use full color or navy logo
- Dark backgrounds: Use white/reversed logo
- Photography: Ensure sufficient contrast, add background if needed
- Patterns: Use only with sufficient clear space

**Color Variations:**
- All logo variations use the same wave pattern colors
- Heart symbol must always be blue in primary versions
- White version: Use heart and text both white on dark backgrounds

### Photography & Imagery

#### Photography Principles

**Authenticity First:**
- Candid over posed whenever possible
- Real members, real moments (with proper written consent)
- Environmental context showing community and connection
- Genuine emotions, not forced smiles

**Representation:**
- Diverse intersections of identity (race, gender, age, ability)
- All ages of foster youth and alumni (14-40+)
- Various settings (urban, suburban, rural)
- Multiple life situations (education, work, family, solo)

**Technical Standards:**
- Natural, warm lighting preferred
- Empowering angles: Eye level or slightly below (not looking down)
- In focus, high resolution (300 DPI for print, 72 DPI for web)
- Maintain natural skin tones in editing
- Minimal filters - authentic over stylized

**Dignity Standards:**
- Show agency, not victimhood
- Forward-facing, aspirational imagery
- Connection and belonging visible
- Professional quality that respects subjects
- Never use "poverty porn" or trauma imagery

#### Image Treatment & Editing

**Acceptable Edits:**
- Brightness/contrast adjustments
- Subtle color grading toward warm tones
- Cropping for composition
- Background blur for focus

**Avoid:**
- Heavy filters or Instagram-style effects
- Overly saturated colors
- Black and white (use sparingly, only for historical context)
- Dramatic shadows or vignettes
- Altering body shapes or facial features

#### Stock Photography Guidelines

When member photos aren't available:

**Selection Criteria:**
- Diverse, authentic representations
- Avoid stereotypical "charity" imagery
- Select empowering, forward-facing poses
- Ensure age-appropriate representation (18-35 range ideal)
- Look for genuine moments, not stock smiles
- Purchase extended licenses for flexibility

**Preferred Stock Sources:**
- Nappy.co (diverse, authentic photography)
- Unsplash (check for authentic, not overly posed)
- Getty Images (premium, diverse selection)
- Avoid: Generic business stock, overly happy/posed stock

#### Image Consent & Rights

**Required for All Member Photography:**
- Written consent before capture
- Specific usage rights defined
- Option to revoke consent
- Name usage preferences documented
- Storage in secure, compliant systems

### Layout & Composition

#### Grid System

**Web/Digital:**
- 12-column grid for desktop (1440px max width)
- 6-column grid for tablet (768px)
- 4-column grid for mobile (375px)
- Consistent margins: 24-48px depending on screen size
- Gutter spacing: 24px
- Modular, flexible sections

**Print:**
- Standard grid based on format
- Generous margins (minimum 0.5 inches)
- Consistent gutters throughout
- Modular layout for flexibility

#### Design Patterns

**Hero Sections:**
- Bold headline (H1) with supporting text
- Strong visual (photo or gradient)
- Clear, prominent CTA
- Breadcrumb or back navigation

**Card Layouts:**
- For programs, resources, team members
- Consistent sizing and spacing
- Clear hierarchy (image → title → description → CTA)
- Subtle shadows for depth

**Testimonial Blocks:**
- Photo + quote + attribution
- Generous padding around text
- Quote marks as visual element
- Name, role, location

**Data Visualizations:**
- Simple, accessible charts (avoid complex graphs)
- Clear labels and legends
- Colorblind-safe color choices
- Text alternative provided

**Icon Systems:**
- Consistent line weight (2-3px)
- Simple, recognizable shapes
- Monochromatic or two-color maximum
- Match visual style to brand

#### White Space Philosophy

**Purpose:**
- Creates visual hierarchy
- Improves readability and comprehension
- Conveys professionalism and quality
- Reduces cognitive load

**Application:**
- Generous padding around all content blocks (24-48px minimum)
- Clear separation between sections
- Breathing room around text (don't crowd)
- Balance between content and space (60/40 rule)

**Avoid:**
- Cramming too much content per page/screen
- Inconsistent spacing (use 8px grid system)
- Filling every pixel
- Unbalanced layouts

### Digital Design Standards

#### Web Design Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance)

**Color & Contrast:**
- Text contrast minimum 4.5:1 (body text)
- Large text minimum 3:1 (18pt+/14pt+ bold)
- UI components minimum 3:1
- Test with colorblindness simulators

**Interactive Elements:**
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px (mobile)
- Click targets minimum 24x24px (desktop)
- Clear focus states for keyboard navigation (visible outline)
- Hover states for all interactive elements
- Disabled states clearly differentiated

**Navigation:**
- Logical tab order
- Skip navigation links
- Breadcrumbs for deep pages
- Clear, descriptive link text (no "click here")

**Forms:**
- Clear, descriptive labels
- Error messages that guide, not blame
- Required fields clearly marked
- Success confirmation messages
- Autocomplete enabled where appropriate

**Media:**
- Alt text for all meaningful images (descriptive, not "image of")
- Captions for all video content
- Transcripts for audio content
- Pause/play controls for auto-playing content

**Responsive Design:**
- Mobile-first approach
- Touch-friendly on all devices
- Readable text without zooming
- No horizontal scrolling
- Optimized load times (<3 seconds)

#### Email Design Standards

**Structure:**
- Single column for mobile optimization
- 600px maximum width
- Clear hierarchy (header → hero → body → CTA → footer)
- Button CTAs (not just text links)

**Technical:**
- Alt text for all images
- Fallback fonts specified
- Preview text optimized (50-90 characters)
- Plain text version provided
- Test across email clients

**Content:**
- Clear subject line (40-50 characters)
- Scannable layout with clear sections
- Primary CTA within first screen
- Unsubscribe link clearly visible

Through our innovative partnership with DGW Branded, we provide sustainable, lifelong support including access to 600,000+ resources, career development through our Staffmark employment pipeline, crisis support, learning workshops, and community events. But most importantly, we provide belonging.

1. Lead with business opportunity (not charity ask)

3. Provide specific partnership outcomes

1. Immediate acknowledgment (don't make them wait)
2. Validate their courage in reaching out
3. Provide specific, actionable options
4. Offer multiple contact methods
5. Reassurance of ongoing support

You deserve support, and we're here to make sure you get exactly what you need. We've helped 1,100+ members navigate similar challenges—you're not alone in this.

Reach out anytime. We've got you."

### Design Decision Framework

When creating new materials, use this decision tree:

**1. Does this reinforce dignity and value?**
- If no: Redesign with empowerment focus, remove deficit framing
- If yes: Continue to step 2

**2. Is this accessible to all audiences?**
- If no: Adjust for WCAG compliance, test with screen readers, check colorblind
- If yes: Continue to step 3

**3. Does this feel authentic to our community?**
- If no: Gather member input, test messaging, revise approach
- If yes: Continue to step 4

**4. Is the quality professional enough for partners and media?**
- If no: Elevate design standards, improve visual hierarchy
- If yes: Continue to step 5

**5. Would members feel proud to share this?**
- If no: Revise until they would (this is the ultimate test)
- If yes: Publish and monitor
